Who We Are

About NMSA

The National Maritime Safety Authority — PNG's primary body ensuring safe, secure, and efficient maritime operations.

NMSA is the statutory authority of Papua New Guinea responsible for maritime safety, marine environment protection, seafarer welfare, and the registration of PNG vessels — safeguarding over 5,200 kilometres of coastline and the livelihoods of thousands of maritime workers.

Our Foundation

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To promote the safety of life at sea, prevent pollution from vessels, and ensure that Papua New Guinea's maritime sector is safe, efficient, and environmentally responsible — for the benefit of all Papua New Guineans.

Our Vision

To be a world-class maritime authority that is trusted, capable, and respected — protecting lives, the marine environment, and the economic prosperity of Papua New Guinea's vast maritime estate.

What We Do

Core Functions

01

Search & Rescue

Operating the MRCC 24/7, coordinating SAR operations across PNG territorial waters, EEZ, and in compliance with SOLAS obligations.

02

Vessel Surveys & Registration

Conducting statutory surveys, granting seaworthiness certificates, and maintaining the PNG Ship Register for all domestic and international flag vessels.

03

Seafarer Certification

Issuing STCW certificates of competency, approving maritime training institutions, and managing the PNG Seafarer Register.

04

Port State Control

Inspecting foreign vessels in PNG ports to verify compliance with international maritime conventions and ensure minimum safety standards.

05

Marine Environment

Implementing MARPOL obligations, overseeing oil spill preparedness, and regulating the discharge of harmful substances from vessels.

06

Aids to Navigation

Maintaining lighthouses, buoys, beacons, and other navigational aids across PNG's extensive coastal and inland waterways network.

Our Journey

NMSA History

1987

NMSA Established

The National Maritime Safety Authority Act was passed, creating NMSA as the principal maritime regulatory body of PNG.

1995

MRCC Commissioned

The Maritime Rescue Coordination Centre in Port Moresby was commissioned, bringing 24/7 SAR coordination to PNG waters.

2004

STCW Compliance

PNG achieved full compliance with the STCW Convention, enabling PNG seafarers to serve on international vessels.

2015

Regional Expansion

Regional offices opened in Lae, Madang, Alotau, Rabaul, and Kimbe, bringing NMSA services closer to maritime communities.

2022

Small Craft Act

The Small Craft Safety Act was enacted, extending NMSA's regulatory reach to the thousands of small vessels operating in PNG waters.

2024

Modernisation Programme

Launch of a major modernisation programme including digital vessel registration, engine room simulator, and upgraded navigational aids.
