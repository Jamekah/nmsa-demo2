About NMSA

The Authority for Maritime Standards in Papua New Guinea

Who We Are

The National Maritime Safety Authority (NMSA) is a statutory authority established by the NMSA Act 2003. Replacing the former Marine Transport Division, we operate as a non-profit government agency dedicated to the safety of life at sea.

Headquartered in Port Moresby with regional presence across the provinces, we ensure that PNG's maritime industry meets both local legislation and international standards set by the IMO (International Maritime Organization).

Our Vision

"To be a proactive leading maritime regulator and administrator committed to safety first at sea and a cleaner marine environment."

Our Mission

To promote PNG's national maritime interests by enforcing legislation, maintaining safety infrastructure, and ensuring compliance with international conventions.

Key Responsibilities

  • Maintenance of Aids to Navigation (Lighthouses & Beacons).
  • Port State Control inspections of foreign vessels.
  • Flag State Control for PNG registered ships.
  • Coordination of Search and Rescue (SAR).
  • Marine Pollution Preparedness and Response.
  • Seafarer Certification and Welfare.

Our Legislation

  • National Maritime Safety Authority Act 2003
  • Merchant Shipping Act 1975
  • Marine Pollution Acts (Various)
  • Small Craft Act 2011
